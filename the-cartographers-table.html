The Cartographer's Table

the Loop, written down for the first time — v1.0 draft, for keeper ratification. The generic, client-agnostic method for producing a living target operating model for any organisation. The models it produces are content; this method is the instrument (PP-04, clause 3). Proven twice as practice — a large public-sector programme and PowerVox itself — and productised here.

"Propose, then correct. The method never asks a client to author their operating model. It proposes an opinionated, partially-wrong model built from the template and their own sources — and harvests the corrections. People cannot write their operating model down. They can correct a wrong one instantly."

Why the method works

Two failure modes kill conventional operating-model work. The consultant's failure: a beautiful document nobody runs — a picture hung on a wall. The questionnaire's failure, in Rachel's words from the founding conversation: you type reams in and get a pretty picture of your own information back. The method beats both with one mechanic. The template arrives already opinionated — five frames, a lifecycle, an actor cast, a system register — so the client is never facing a blank page. The engine populates it from their real sources — documents, a recorded discovery call, their website — so the first draft is concrete enough to be wrong in specific, correctable ways. And the engagement is then a correction loop, because the two most informative events in any build are the moments the client says "no — that's not a product, that's a process" or "those two things aren't siblings, one sits inside the other". The corrections are the model. Everything else is furniture.

The engagement · three months, three movements
Month one
Discovery

Source inventory gathered. The recorded discovery call — one to two hours, run by us, expert personas loaded into the question frame per section, transcribed under consent. The engine ingests everything and mirrors it back: "here is what your organisation says it is — correct us."

Month two
Build & review

The skeleton model proposed — clickable, navigable, deliberately complete enough to be wrong. Correction loops in structured sessions: classifications visible, the client's eye as ground truth, every settlement papered as it lands.

Month three
Iterate & hand over

Depth earned where corrections have settled; the gap audit run honestly (what came from your sources, what we proposed, what still lives in someone's head); the external-readiness pass; hand-over of the model — theirs to run, with the cadence to run it by.

The eight moves · the elicitation playbook

Ingest sources in, nothing asserted

Everything the client has — strategy papers, org charts, contracts, the website, prior consultancy — read in full before a single question is asked. Facts are traced to sources from the first hour; what has no source is flagged, not assumed.

Instrument: the source inventory checklist; the provenance register that every later artefact inherits.

Mirror the synthesis that invites correction

The engine plays back what the sources say the organisation is — its purpose, its products, its actors, its rhythms — as a confident, specific synthesis. The mirror is not a summary; it is bait. The first corrections it draws are the most valuable data of the engagement.

Instrument: the mirror-back document; the discovery call script, with expert-persona frames per section (financial, technical, regulatory, people) so each domain is interrogated in its own language.

Skeleton the opinionated first model

The five-question structure proposed and populated: WHY at the centre, WHAT as the wares, WHEN as the loop or lifecycle the business actually lives by, HOW as the operational floor, WHO as the named cast. One lifecycle, run across product configurations — never one model per product.

Instrument: the template kit — front door, hubs, operational matrix with configuration filters, system register, persona pages.

Correct the loop that makes it true

Structured correction sessions with every classification visible — products as chips, processes as lanes, actors as cards — because subjects cannot author taxonomies but correct them on sight. Small option sets with recommendations, never open questions. The client's screen is ground truth; their markup outranks the engine's estimates, always.

Instrument: the markup loop; structured choice points; the deliberate classification tests (products v processes, parent v subsidiary, model v method).

Settle rulings become constitution

When the sponsor rules on something architectural, it is papered immediately: one position, its reasons, and the triggers that would reopen it. Constitutions accumulate from rulings. The engine's job is to notice a ruling has happened and offer the paper while the reasoning is warm.

Instrument: the position-paper template; the client lexicon; the bridge brief stating which artefact is authoritative on what; the allocation test for two-altitude organisations.

Deepen detail where it has been earned

Depth follows settlement, never precedes it. Operational walkthroughs, system designs, persona pages and governance shapes are built only on corrected foundations — and every system design the engine proposes where the client's sources are silent carries the proposal flag and an owner to confirm it.

Instrument: the system register pattern (flow, tooling, artefacts, failure modes per system); expert modules producing flagged, reviewable artefacts with a named human gate.

Audit the honest progress meter

The standing report: what is populated from your sources, what is proposed and flagged, what is blocked on a named human. This triage is the engagement's truth — it cannot be gamed, and it tells the client exactly what their model still needs from them.

Instrument: the gap audit; the status meta-view with visible counts, every item owned and deep-linked.

Handoff a model that runs

The external-readiness pass strips the scaffolding; the cadence rules are encoded so the model is lived through, not framed; the engagement state is written down so any future session — theirs or ours — resumes cold. The model is the client's. The method, the templates and the anonymised pattern stay with PowerVox.

Instrument: the strip pass; the cadence encoding; the engagement state pack; the pattern-contribution clause (Deep Vault, class B).
What the template carries · the primitives
Five frames

WHY at the centre; WHAT, WHEN, HOW, WHO around it. The elicitation frame and the navigation are the same structure.

Two nested loops (WHEN)

An inner cadence (the rhythm of doing) inside an outer renewal (measure → improve). Every model has both; the louder one is set by where the business wins or loses, and can shift over time. The stages are elicited, never imposed.

Configurations

One lifecycle × N products with filter highlighting. Kills the duplication that bloats conventional TOMs.

The named cast

Actors as people with portraits, protections and "why this role exists" panels. Models get adopted when their actors are human.

Spine and overlay

Universal versus configurable, made visible by colour — the primitive that lets one platform serve many configurations.

The artefact stack

Front door → places → operational walkthroughs → system register → canonical sources. Five levels of drill-down, all cross-linked.

Status discipline

Resolved / in progress / unresolved with visible counts; honesty furniture throughout — chips, flags, dashed not-yets, launch gates.

The governance pack

A client lexicon, position papers with reopen triggers, the bridge brief, one canonical home per fact.

The optional skin

A narrative world over conventional bones, with a named canon owner — offered where the client has a story, never invented uninvited.

The leadership lens · what the WHO and HOW surface

One method, two readings

The five frames map the whole operating model. Two of them carry a second reading that is the bridge to the rest of PowerVox: WHO holds the power and HOW the place actually runs. Pointed at leadership, those frames surface the conditions of the room — where power sits, what the room is built to receive, who is capable and unheard inside it, and whether it is willing to change. That structured read is the Conditions Taxonomy, twelve dimensions in four groups. It is not a separate engagement and it does not compete with the five frames; it is what this one method reveals when it is run on a leadership problem. And it is the reason the Loop is the front door: map any organisation honestly and its leadership and communication gaps appear on their own, which is exactly the demand the Greenhouse and the allyship pathway are built to meet. The Loop is the master method; the taxonomy is one of the things it brings to the surface.

Proof status, held honestly. The template layer is proven on two real subjects. The elicitation engine is not yet proven on the hard case: both builds had rich sources and an expert in the room. PowerVox is the rehearsal (it proves the template populates), a paid validation client the validation, a zero-knowledge client the falsification test — a cold start. Until that test reports, this method claims craft, not magic. Metrics defined before the engagements begin: cells populated from sources versus proposed; correction cycles to settlement; client hours per stack level; positions papered; pattern-library entries of reusable quality.

What the client gets, and what stays with us

Theirs: the operating model itself — a clickable, navigable artefact they host and run (a live hosted version as a premium add-on), with the cadence and status discipline that make it a working tool rather than a wall picture. Ours: the method, the template kit, the elicitation instruments, and an anonymised, organisation-unidentifiable pattern contributed to the library under the engagement's contribution clause — the asset that compounds, so every engagement makes the next one sharper. Engagement shape: three months, expert-led, £ — (pricing confirmation at PP-05 ratification).
