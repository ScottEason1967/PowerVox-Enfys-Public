The Black Egg

The Black Egg measures one thing: the distance between what a leader thinks they said and what a reader actually receives. Not a writing tool. It exists because in high-stakes environments the failure mode is not bad writing — it is filtered truth.

Know when your language changes what happens next.
A polite message can score high if it transmits. A "nice" message can score low if it distorts.
v1 shows you the truth. v2 shows you yourself. v3 shows you the system.

What it measures

Five properties of transmission fidelity: is the core claim present and unhidden; is someone accountable and named; are stakes and timing visible; is there a decision, an ask or a next step — or only a wish; and how much of the message is diluted by distorting language. A high score does not mean "well written". It means a reader will take the message you intended, at the weight you intended, with the action you intended. The commercial defensibility, in the spec's own words: grammar tools are commodity; transmission fidelity — the gap between intent and impact — is a leadership problem, and nothing in the market names it cleanly.

The architectural rule that runs through the whole spec: score is structural, context is interpretive. The Context Envelope (audience × purpose, inferred silently and correctable) never changes a score — it changes which risks are surfaced and how the rewrite speaks. "Let me know your thoughts" passes in Inform → Peer and fails in Decide → Senior Leaders, at the same score.

Free · acquisition

Stateless single-message engine: paste or dictate (3 min voice, transcribed, user-approved), click, uncomfortable truth. Five-dimension scoring, Message Gap, rewrite. No login, no storage. Audio discarded in real time, ZDR — never stored.

Job: prove truth · viral, demoable

The founder-level rules the spec locks

Consent is not payment. Consent unlocks memory; payment unlocks intelligence. The opt-in moment must be visibly separate from the upgrade moment — frame consent as a consequence of payment and enterprise trust erodes. No tier contamination. Every feature names its tier; cross-tier features are rejected by default — v2 features sneaking into v1 is the named failure mode, and holding that line under sales pressure is the moat.
