What it is

The destination, not a stop on the way. Root to Grow plants; the Greenhouse is where leaders stay to keep growing. Members enter a monthly rhythm of practice and presence, and re-take the diagnostic each quarter. That single habit is what turns a one-off beta snapshot into change-over-time data on a held cohort — the same people, measured again and again, which is the kind of evidence the category needs and the kind almost nobody else has. It is the relational heart of the work and the practice ground where the compensation hypothesis keeps being tested against real leaders under real pressure.

Commercially it is the recurring engine. The plan assumes ~60% of Root to Grow alumni convert — a conservative planning figure, ⚑ unproven until real cohorts run — paying from the end of their cohort. The in-year figure is modest because members join through the year, but that misreads the asset. What matters is the annual run-rate it is exiting on: roughly £ —carried into the next year before a single new cohort runs. Priced for longevity, not volume. The run-rate leans on a second assumption worth naming: ~85% annual retention ⚑ — watched alongside the conversion figure above, because the asset story stands or falls on members staying.

Why women, why now

The Greenhouse backs women leaders because the ground is moving under exactly that group, and the move is structural rather than a fashion. The largest wealth transfer in history is underway and it is flowing toward women, on four converging drivers McKinsey calls structural: women marrying later, earning and graduating more, outliving male spouses, and growing in financial confidence. It does not reverse on a cycle.

The last figure is the one that matters most for the mission. Income that reaches children through the mother has a larger positive causal effect on their cognitive, health and economic outcomes than the same income reaching the household another way, on Cooper and Stewart's 2020 systematic review of RCTs and longitudinal studies. So money in women's hands does not simply sit there, it compounds into the next generation. Backing the women who will hold that wealth, and helping them lead with a voice that lands, is the wedge the Greenhouse is built on. One honest boundary: the dossier establishes the stakes, not the mechanism — the claim that voice capability is how these leaders capture the moment is PowerVox's own hypothesis, and proving it is the Observatory's job, not this page's.

Why the ruthless rise first — and why that is the Greenhouse's case, not its objection. The dossier's hardest chapter says the quiet part out loud: competitive environments reward coherence, not virtue. The ruthless rise first because they are undivided — no internal voice second-guessing, no energy bled through shame — while good people leak the very coherence the environment responds to, through self-doubt, hyper-reflection and shame: the same leaks the construct names. The chapter's answer is not to become ruthless. It is that coherence and goodness are not opposites — accountability without shame builds character where shame destroys it — and a good leader restored to coherence outcompetes the ruthless one, because they are no longer fighting themselves. That is precisely the work: the Greenhouse exists to give good leaders the coherence the ruthless get for free. From the Hearts & Minds research companion, "Why the Ruthless Rise First."

The other half · changing the room

The Greenhouse re-waters the plant. It does not, on its own, end the drought. A capable woman whose voice is restored still walks back into a room built to mishear her: direct heard as “gobby”, conviction heard as “emotional”. By PowerVox's own analysis the conditions are the variable, not the person, so the honest model has a second pathway aimed at the room itself. The allyship pathway works with the people who hold the power so they learn to receive what they currently mishear. It is gender-neutral, it can run from day one where an organisation wants it, and it stays a distinct pathway from the women-only one. It is the half that changes the drought rather than re-watering a single plant, and it is what makes the offer true rather than complicit.

The allyship pathway

Teaches the power-holders in the room to receive differently. Gender-neutral, and the destination the whole offer is heading toward: rooms that receive truth whoever speaks it.

The readiness gate. One honest fork decides which engagement is real: are the people who hold the power willing to learn to receive differently? If yes, the two pathways run together and the change holds after we leave. If not yet, we will still run the Greenhouse honestly, but we name plainly that you are re-watering a plant you are sending back into drought, and the ripple is then carried by her. Women-only spaces stay necessary now, because there are things sayable in that room that cannot be said in a mixed one. The destination is gender-neutral, because the answer is equality. Women now because the drought falls hardest on them now. Said openly, that sequencing is the strategy, not a hedge, and it is why PowerVox is a company that maps conditions, not a women's-empowerment company: the women's cohort is the first intervention the map surfaces, not the headline.

The cost it carries, stated honestly

The Greenhouse is not free to run. It costs roughly 3–4 founder-days a month while it is being designed and embedded, settling to 1–2 days a month within about three months. That growing monthly load is the real reason the year holds at five cohorts rather than more: two people cannot run extra cohorts and an expanding membership on one calendar. The Year 2–3 unlock is direct membership without a cohort first — opened on a trigger, not a date, once the rhythm is grooved and the run-rate justifies it.
