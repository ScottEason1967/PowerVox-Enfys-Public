The Cast of Enfys

The model has a human face. In Rachel's Enfys story, four children carry the parts of how a person lands or fails to land in others, so the framework can be felt as a tale before it is learned as a model. Each child is also a leader you will recognise.

Three intelligences, and the star they are read by.

The story turns on the Hum: the shared frequency of meaning, feeling and truth. On the planet Treah it is the baseline everyone lives in. On Earth it has not been destroyed, only buried under noise, speed and disconnection, people trained out of hearing it. A boy is sent to where they have forgotten how. The way back is not magic; it is learning to regulate, notice, make meaning and express. Treah and Earth are the integrated system and the fragmented one, the same contrast a leader meets between a room that holds them and one that does not.

Will

VQ · Voice
also Aynil, the boy from Treah, the one who is sent

Will arrives from Treah, a world still tuned to the Hum, into an Earth that has lost it. Where others are looked at, he sees the person, the one the room has made invisible. He becomes the integrator the others organise around, then absorbs too much: the grief, the indifference, the weight of holding a field alone. His voice goes quiet, the crack, until the others come for him and bring him back.

The leader he is

The one who stops speaking. Burnout and overload, what happens when voice meets a system it was not built for, and why silence in a leader is not rest, it is system failure, and how to come back from it.

Ada

IQ · Intellect
the observing mind, the notebook, the pattern-spotter

Ada watches, patterns and tests. She sees the structure of a problem before anyone names it, and her notebook is where unresolved pattern is held. Her identity as "the clever one" turns out to be too small for her; across the story the notebook grows from data-collection into real understanding, of Will, of Ceri, and of herself.

The leader she is

The one who analyses everything and trusts nothing they cannot measure. High IQ without feeling, the illusion of control, and why the smartest person in the room is sometimes the least effective communicator.

Ceri

EQ · Feeling
the one who feels everything, the listening post

Ceri feels too much and has no language for it. He masks at school, regulates by breathing and walking, and keeps a private hollow as his safe place. He senses the atmosphere of a room, the tension, the shame, the sadness nobody has said. Reframed through the story, he is not "too sensitive", he is finely tuned, a listening post whose gift is a function, not a burden.

The leader he is

The one who carries too much. High EQ without integration, feeling everything and paying for it, and how emotional salience becomes overload without language and boundaries.

Seren

Signal · the receptive
the one who notices, the mirror

Seren is eight. She sings about muffins and miracles, notices Ceri's breathing without judgement, and meets Will knowing at once that he is different, and finding that interesting rather than strange. She is woven quietly through the story until, when Will is gone, she becomes the connector the others cannot do without. Seren is the mirror: she sees what everyone else has stopped seeing.

The leader she is

The perception we all had at eight and were trained out of. How professional life suppresses Signal, rewarding certainty over curiosity, speed over attention, data over instinct.

Signal is the receptive dimension within Voice Quotient™, not a fourth intelligence beside IQ, EQ and VQ. The story gives it a face; the model keeps it inside VQ.

More of the village, to come

The story carries a wider cast the site has not yet drawn: Liayn on Treah; Mr Morgan and the bench he keeps for Elenor; Mrs Davies, the post-office woman nobody sees; Nia and the hospital; and the Shimmer, the messenger who appears. They join as the Hearts and Minds layer is built out.

On the portraits. The four faces are painted in the same hand and world as the village, each in their place with their tell: Will with his hand on his chest, Ada with her notebook, Ceri in his green hollow, Seren reaching for the rainbow at the fountain. A first set for Rachel to keep or refine.
