What it is

The quiet, premium end of the founder's work. The White Sofa is Rachel's one-to-one coaching: founder-led, premium, and held deliberately small. It is the intimate counterpart to speaking. Where a keynote puts the category in front of a room, the sofa is a single high-trust relationship with one leader, in private. It is a genuine paid service and a real revenue line, Rachel's own stated priority, but it is a founder service rather than a productised offer, because it cannot be delivered without Rachel in the room. That is the whole point of it, and also the reason it stays capped.

It is pointed, not open-ended. Every coaching relationship routes back to the diagnostic and the wider work, so the sofa feeds the funnel rather than becoming a standalone bespoke book. It earns at the top of the market and it deepens the founder's authority, but it is run as a deliberately limited seat, not a line to scale.

The coaching routes back to the diagnostic and the cohorts. It deepens authority and demand rather than growing as a separate line of its own.

The sofa and the stage are two different things

It is worth keeping these apart. Speaking is part of the village's signal — the founder's voice creating recognition and drawing people to the gate. It lives with the wider Hearts and Minds work, not here. Coaching is the White Sofa: the private, one-to-one service. Folding them into a single "speaking and coaching" line blurs a channel that generates demand with a service that delivers it. The sofa is the service. The stage is the signal.
