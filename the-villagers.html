The Villagers

A client lane wants a face. Each ware gets its own villager: who they are, what they want, what they fear, where they enter, and what done looks like. Settled as the persona-per-product rule; the names and faces await Rachel's pen ⚑ — the epithets below are working titles, the situations are drawn from the product pages and the plan.

?

The Curious First-Timer

the Black Egg
⚑ name awaits Rachel's pen · the one at the crier's call

A capable leader who heard the signal — a talk, a piece, a colleague — and tried the free instrument with a real message. The result named something they had half-known for years: what they send is not what lands.

What they want

To understand the gap. Why careful messages come back misread, why the room hears a different person than the one speaking.

What they fear

That the problem is them; that naming it out loud makes it real to others before they have a handle on it.

Where they enter

The town crier on the path: the free Egg, then the paid tier when it ships — the first honest line they write about how they land.

Done looks like: the gap named in their own data, a Voice Profile in hand, and the free-to-paid step taken — warm and qualified by the time Root to Grow is mentioned.

The Cohort Leader

Root to Grow
⚑ name awaits Rachel's pen · the one in the seedbed

Senior enough that the stakes are real, funded enough to say yes, and timed to a cohort — the routing rules in person. Joins ten or eleven others for six weeks of live work with Rachel and Hannah.

What they want

A voice that lands under consequence: in the board pack moment, the difficult conversation, the room that outranks them.

What they fear

That speaking plainly costs them standing; that the version of themselves who survives meetings is the only one colleagues will ever meet.

Where they enter

The Gate, warm and qualified; baseline VQ Assessment and Black Egg at the Welcome, the start of their own line.

Done looks like: measurable movement against their own baseline, anchors that travel home, and the week-five conversation about staying — into the Greenhouse — answered from experience, not persuasion.

The Alumna

the Greenhouse
⚑ name awaits Rachel's pen · the one who stayed

Finished the cohort and chose not to drift. She is the person the wedge is built for: a woman leading through the decade the ground moves toward women, who knows six weeks starts something it cannot finish.

What they want

A held place to keep practising: the monthly rhythm, the rooms where courage is rehearsed, peers who have each other's backs.

What they fear

Sliding back alone; becoming the leader the old environment kept asking her to be.

Where they enter

From cohort-end, paying monthly; the quarterly re-measure makes her growth visible and the dataset longitudinal.

Done looks like: she renews because the practice holds, her line keeps moving, and in time she becomes the proof — the case study (her separate consent), the referral, the voice that draws the next villager in.

The Sponsor

the Loop · Groundwork ⚑
⚑ name awaits Rachel's pen · the buyer at the workshop barn

An organisational buyer — the founder, COO or CPO of a business that runs on what lives in a few heads. Commissions the three-month operating-model engagement because something is coming: growth, a sale, a handover, or just the limit of heroics.

What they want

The business out of the founders' heads and onto a map a stranger could run: visible, honest, theirs to keep.

What they fear

Consultancy that leaves slideware and a bill; a model nobody opens twice; their people's candour ending up somewhere it shouldn't.

Where they enter

The enterprise road or a warm referral; contract with the pattern-contribution clause, discovery with every voice consented before recording.

Done looks like: an operating model their team runs without the consultants in the room — the model theirs, the method ours — and a sponsor who can show, not describe, how the business works.
?

The Coachee

the White Sofa ⚑
⚑ name awaits Rachel's pen · the one on the intimate seat

A founder or executive whose stakes are too particular for a cohort: the exit, the boardroom rupture, the speech that decides something. Buys the private room, and Rachel in it.

What they want

The highest-stakes version of the work, one to one: their actual situation, their actual rooms, nothing generalised.

What they fear

Exposure. Being seen working on the thing before it is fixed.

Where they enter

By referral and fit, capped deliberately — a founder service, not a productised one, every relationship routing back to the diagnostic.

Done looks like: the moment met — the room moved — and a leader who knows exactly which instrument told them why.
What this page is, and is not. These are the chassis's client avatars: who each ware serves, grounded in the product pages and the plan.
