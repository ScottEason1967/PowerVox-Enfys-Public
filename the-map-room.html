The reframe the decade rests on.

The original research framing — "validate VQ as a leadership framework" — positioned PowerVox inside the crowded, procurement-resistant leadership-communication market, where the buyer is a Head of L&D and the competitor set is every coaching firm with a presence framework. The adopted reframe moves PowerVox into organisational health and human sustainability: a faster-growing market, a different buyer (CPO, Chief Wellbeing Officer, sometimes the CFO via attrition cost), and critically under-served by measurable, methodology-led interventions. The wellbeing industry sells outcomes without mechanism; the leadership-development industry sells mechanism without outcomes. PowerVox sells both, joined — the only player with a mechanistic answer to the question CPOs actually have: which specific, trainable leadership behaviours measurably reduce burnout risk and raise psychological safety?

Three things make the hypothesis powerful: it is mechanistic, not correlational — each link in the chain is independently measurable, a publishable study, and a coaching intervention point; it contributes where the psychological-safety literature is weakest — behaviours like "ask for input" are downstream of receptive capacity, and a leader can perform them and still destroy safety if their Signal is poor, because the team feels unread; and it inverts the burnout conversation — leader burnout as a compensation pattern driven by deteriorating relational and perceptual capacity, not workload alone, which makes it trainable rather than a workload-management problem.
