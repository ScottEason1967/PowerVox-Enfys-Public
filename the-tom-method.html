What it is

The second engine. The Loop is paid delivery of a target operating model and the work that surrounds it. The deeper point is that it is the consulting arm's version of the move the rest of PowerVox is making: founder-dependent delivery becoming a repeatable asset. The instrument is what makes that possible — a structured way to elicit, design and hand over an operating model that does not live only in the founders' heads. The method itself is the third thing in the estate: not the Loveheart ecology and not the PowerVox chassis, but the instrument both can pick up.

It proves itself across three kinds of client at once. PowerVox is the rehearsal subject — we run the method on ourselves first. A paid validation client is the validation. And a zero-knowledge client is the falsification test: what happens when the method meets a context the founders know nothing about, starting cold. That spread is the design, not an accident of who turned up.

Where it sits

The Loop is part of PowerVox. PowerVox Ltd contracts for it, the IP stays in-house, and today, while it is still founder-delivered, its revenue sits on the bespoke side of the product-share metric, not the product side. The Loop is built to migrate to product as the method externalises through the question set and the pattern library, and that crossing is itself part of what lifts the share upward. The working price is £ —. The trade name is still to be settled, so the product is described here by what it does rather than by a name not yet given.

Its place in the village

The Loop is the workshop barn — where things are built to be used again, not made once and forgotten. It is the second of the two engines: the funnel scales the product, the barn scales the craft. Both are the same idea, that delivery should become an asset rather than a debt against founder time.
