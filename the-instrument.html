The method core

The Instrument

Every engagement runs on the same spine. This page is the part that does not change from one client to the next. The fixed core is the product; everything a client tells us is the variable it runs on. Draw the line between those two cleanly and the rest of the business follows.

Whether a voice lands = the voice × the room

A voice that lands is not a property of the speaker alone. It is what the speaker transmits meeting what the room is built to receive. Same voice, two rooms, two outcomes. So the thing we are really working on has two variables, not one: the voice, and the room. Most of the market works only on the first. The whole method is built to work on both, with one honest asymmetry: we can raise the voice directly, but the room we cannot control, only map and work with the organisation to improve, so the voice can be optimised within it.

The Canon · what is always true

Eight invariants. They hold for every client, in every sector, regardless of who is in the room. Each carries its boundary, because a claim without a boundary is the fastest way to lose a serious reader. This is the spec, not the taxonomy: it does not flex per engagement.

Axiom 1
The room is half the problem

A voice that lands is the voice meeting the room. Locating the entire problem in the speaker, then fixing only the speaker, is incomplete by our own analysis.

Boundary  This does not excuse the speaker. Both variables are real; we work the one the client will let us reach.
Axiom 2
The conditions are the variable, not the person

Same person, same voice, same hour: supplicant in one room, authority in the next. What changed was who held the power, and what that power was built to receive.

Boundary  Capability still has to be present. We make a present capability legible; we do not manufacture one that is not there.
Axiom 3
Capability is usually misread, not missing

The common failure is a capable voice the room recodes: direct heard as “aggressive”, conviction as “emotional”, brevity as “not ready”.

Boundary  Where capability is genuinely absent, that is development, a different engagement, and we say so.
Axiom 4
Two levers, and only two

Change the speaker’s state so the voice becomes available, or change the room so it receives differently. Durable change usually needs the second.

Boundary  The first lever alone, used repeatedly, sends a capable person back into the same drought and calls it progress.
Axiom 5
The voice is measured; the room is mapped

Voice Quotient scores the speaker across six dimensions. The Conditions Taxonomy maps the room across its own. The diagnosis is the interaction between the two.

Boundary  Neither half is the whole instrument. A VQ score with no map of the room is a number without its context.
Axiom 6
We measure capability change, not outcomes

VQ before and after is the claim we stand behind. Ripple, growth and organisational outcomes are studied and hoped for, never sold.

Boundary  Nothing counts as evidence until it clears a threshold set before the data arrives. That line is what stops the category over-claiming.
Axiom 7
The instrument is gender-neutral; the first cohort is not

The method reads any room. The most urgent and most fundable first intervention is the women’s cohort, because the drought falls hardest there now. The destination is equality: rooms that receive truth whoever speaks it.

Boundary  Said openly, the sequencing is the strategy, not a hedge.
Axiom 8
The honesty is load-bearing

We will not simply harden a capable person, return them to an unchanged room, and call it done. Naming that plainly is what makes the offer true rather than complicit.

Boundary  A client may still buy only the voice work. We take it, and we name the limit of what it can do alone.
The two instruments

The Canon resolves into two working instruments, one for each variable. PowerVox has spent its depth on the first. The second is the half the rest of the market leaves out, and the half this estate now adds.

Instrument one · the voice

Voice Quotient™

A measurable, six-dimension capacity spanning what a leader transmits and what they receive: Presence, Signal, Clarity, Authenticity, Conviction, Impact. It scores the speaker. It is the deep, validated heart of the method and the thing competitors do not measure on the receptive side.

The full architecture lives in the Construct and PP-01.
Instrument two · the room

The Conditions Taxonomy

A fixed schema of the dimensions along which rooms differ: where power sits, what the room is built to receive, who is capable and unheard, and whether the room is willing to change. It maps the room. The client supplies the values; we own the schema.

Set out in full below, and in PP-07.

VQ scores the speaker. The Conditions Taxonomy maps the room. The diagnosis is where a high-VQ voice meets a low-reception room, and that gap is exactly what we are hired to close.

How this sits with the Loop. Two instruments are not an engagement. What a client actually buys is the Loop, the method that maps a whole operating model across five frames (why, what, when, how, who) and harvests the corrections. The Loop is the master method and the delivery vehicle; both instruments ride inside it. VQ scores the people the Loop names in the WHO. The Conditions Taxonomy is the leadership-and-voice lens the Loop runs within its WHO and HOW, not a second method competing with the five frames. Map any organisation honestly and its leadership and communication gaps surface on their own, which is precisely what makes the Loop the front door to the offerings those gaps call for.
The Conditions Taxonomy · v0.1

Twelve dimensions in four groups. This is a fixed schema with variable values: the headings never change, the contents are filled fresh for every client. Because the schema is fixed, every engagement deposits a comparable row, which is what turns bespoke delivery into a compounding library rather than a string of one-offs.

AWhere the power sits — who the room is actually built around
Decision rightsA1
Who actually decides, as against who is named as deciding. The two are often different people.
Access to the roomA2
Who is present at the moment the decision is made, and who only hears the outcome.
Convening powerA3
Who can call the meeting, set the agenda and decide when it ends.
BWhat the room receives — the reception the voice has to survive
Mishearing patternB1
How the room recodes signals: direct read as aggressive, conviction as emotional, brevity as junior, warmth as soft.
Credibility currencyB2
What the room treats as proof: volume, certainty, seniority, tenure, data, the right accent.
Permission structureB3
What may be said, by whom, and what is effectively unsayable however true.
CThe capable and unheard — the cost the room is already paying
Who grows through cracksC1
The capable people the room currently mishears, named by role and pattern rather than blame.
Cost of mishearingC2
What the organisation loses: attrition, withheld ideas, worse decisions, the deal that needed the other voice in the room.
Voice availabilityC3
Whether present capability is reaching the room or stalling before it, at perception, integration, expression or landing.
DReadiness to change the room — the fork that decides the engagement
Power-holder willingnessD1
Will the people who hold the power learn to receive differently? This is the readiness gate, and it decides which engagement is honest.
Budget locusD2
Where the money and the mandate already sit, often a retention, development or inclusion line that is looking for somewhere real to land.
Sponsor honestyD3
Does the sponsor want the room changed, or only the person hardened? Named at the outset, never assumed.

⚑ v0.1, the Cartographer’s first cut. The schema is deliberately small enough to elicit in one engagement and stable enough to compare across many. Widen only with a reason; every new dimension is a cost paid by every future map.

The line · where our IP stops and the client’s particulars begin

This is the single most valuable boundary in the business. Everything to the left is ours and compounds across every client. Everything to the right is the client’s, elicited fresh and disposable. The line itself is the product, because it is what a client is buying that they could not rebuild alone.

Why the line is the moat. Because the taxonomy schema is fixed, the map of every client is comparable to the map of every other. After a handful of engagements you can say something no competitor can say without the same handful: organisations of this shape reliably show this gap, and this intervention reliably moves it. That sentence is the asset. The Loop turns founder time into rows in the library; the library turns rows into a claim; the claim is what eventually sells without the founder in the room.
